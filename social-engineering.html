🧠 Social Engineering

Scam Types

  • Fake Tech Support: Callers pretending to be from Microsoft/Google ask to access your system remotely.
  • Fake Government/Police Calls: Scare tactics used to demand payments or personal details.
  • Impersonation Messages: Fraudsters pretend to be relatives, teachers, or officials and ask for urgent money/help.

Tips to Stay Safe

  • Do not trust unsolicited calls, pop-ups, or emails asking for action urgently.
  • Always verify the identity of unknown callers using a second contact method.
  • Never share OTPs, passwords, or personal details over phone or message.
  • Report suspicious or threatening communication immediately.

How to Report a Social Engineering Scam

📞 Call the Cybercrime Helpline: 1930

🌐 Report Online:

  1. Visit https://cybercrime.gov.in
  2. Click on “File a Complaint”
  3. Select “Report Other Cyber Crime”
  4. Log in with your mobile number and OTP
  5. Upload screenshots or audio recordings if available

Stay Smart, Not Scared

Social engineering works by playing with your emotions — urgency, fear, or trust. The best defense is calm thinking and verification. When in doubt, hang up or ignore. Think before you trust!
