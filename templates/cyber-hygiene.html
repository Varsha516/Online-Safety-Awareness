📊 Cyber Hygiene & Prevention

Best Practices for Staying Safe Online

  • Use strong, unique passwords: Avoid common names or birth dates. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of protection to your accounts.
  • Think before you click: Avoid suspicious links, popups, and unknown attachments.
  • Update regularly: Keep apps, browsers, and OS up to date with the latest security patches.
  • Backup data: Store important data in secure cloud services or external drives.
  • Use antivirus software: Install and update antivirus/firewall tools to protect your device.

Quick Self-Check

  • Do you reuse the same password on multiple sites?
  • Have you shared your OTP or personal info recently?
  • Are your apps asking unnecessary permissions?
  • Is your browser using HTTPS connections?

If any answer is yes — it's time to improve your cyber hygiene!

Reporting a Cyber Incident

If you suspect your account was compromised, personal info leaked, or you clicked on a suspicious link, follow these steps:

  1. Go to https://cybercrime.gov.in
  2. Click on “File a Complaint”
  3. Select the appropriate option (e.g., “Report Other Cyber Crime”)
  4. Log in with your mobile number and submit the form with screenshots/evidence

You can also call the national cybercrime helpline at 📞 1930.

Stay Alert, Stay Safe

Practicing cyber hygiene is like brushing your teeth — it may seem small, but it's crucial for preventing bigger problems. Stay alert, think twice before clicking, and always protect your digital footprint.
