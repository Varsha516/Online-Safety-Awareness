💸 Financial Frauds

Common Types of Financial Frauds

  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites pretending to be banks or services asking for login info.
  • Smishing: Fraudulent SMS messages with malicious links to steal financial data.
  • Vishing: Voice calls pretending to be from bank/officials asking for card or OTP info.
  • Credit/Debit Card Fraud: Unauthorized use of your card data for purchases.
  • SIM Swap Fraud: Criminals duplicate your SIM to intercept OTPs and access bank accounts.
  • QR Code Scams: Fraudsters send QR codes that debit money when scanned, instead of crediting it.

Tips to Stay Safe

  • Never share OTPs, passwords, or CVV — no genuine bank ever asks for it.
  • Do not scan random QR codes sent by unknown persons.
  • Always verify the URL of banking websites (look for https:// and lock icon).
  • Set transaction limits on UPI and net banking apps.
  • Use official banking apps and check for app permissions.

What to Do If You're a Victim

  • Call your bank immediately and request to block the account/card.
  • Call the cybercrime helpline: 📞 1930 (available 24x7)
  • Forward phishing emails to report.phishing@rbi.org.in
  • Keep screenshots and transaction IDs as evidence.

Report Financial Fraud Online

🌐 Visit the official portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in

  1. Click on “File a Complaint”
  2. Select “Report Other Cyber Crime”
  3. Log in using your mobile number and OTP
  4. Fill out the form and upload any evidence (screenshots, messages, transaction ID)

Stay Informed

Financial fraud can happen to anyone — even tech-savvy users. Stay vigilant, avoid emotional decisions under pressure, and always double-check before making online transactions. Awareness is your first line of defense.
