🔐 Personal Threats

Common Threats

  • Identity Theft: Stealing someone's personal data (name, Aadhaar, PAN, etc.) and using it fraudulently.
  • Cyberbullying: Intentionally hurting someone using digital platforms through insults, threats, or harassment.
  • Sextortion: Using private images/videos to threaten victims and demand money or more content.
  • Cyberstalking: Continuously tracking, threatening, or spying on someone online without consent.

Awareness & Prevention Tips

  • Always keep your social media profiles private and share content cautiously.
  • Never share passwords, OTPs, or sensitive photos with anyone — even if they claim to be a friend.
  • If someone is threatening or blackmailing you, block them immediately and talk to a trusted adult or authority.
  • Use security settings and parental controls where applicable.
  • Keep evidence like screenshots or message logs in case you need to report.

Reporting & Support

📞 Helpline: Call 1930 (National Cybercrime Support)

🧑‍🏫 Inform your parents, teacher, or school counselor immediately if you're under threat.

📱 Block and report the person on the platform (Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook).

🌐 Report online at:

  • Cybercrime Portal – Women/Child Reporting Form

📝 Keep a copy of your complaint reference ID for follow-up.

Be Aware

Digital safety starts with awareness. Don’t ignore red flags — harassment, threats, or identity misuse online should never be taken lightly. Speak up early and report confidently. You are not alone — help is available.
